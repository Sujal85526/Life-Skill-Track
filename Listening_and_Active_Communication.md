## Listening and Active Communication

### Q1. Steps/strategies for active listening
- Pause other tasks, face the speaker, and maintain open body language.
- Listen without interrupting; focus on the words, tone, and feelings.
- Paraphrase key points to confirm understanding before responding.

### Q2. Fisher’s reflective listening points
- Show that you heard the message by restating it in simple words.
- Check the speaker’s feelings and meaning with gentle follow-up questions.
- Keep the spotlight on the speaker’s experience instead of offering quick fixes.

### Q3. Obstacles in my listening process
- Getting distracted by phones or multitasking.
- Jumping to conclusions before hearing the full message.
- Emotional triggers that make me defensive.

### Q4. How I can improve my listening
- Put devices away and take brief notes to stay present.
- Use short verbal cues (“I see,” “go on”) to encourage sharing.
- Ask clarifying questions instead of assuming.

### Q5. When I switch to passive communication
- In group settings where I fear conflict.
- When I feel underprepared or unsure about the topic.

### Q6. When I switch to aggressive communication
- When I feel rushed and someone slows me down.
- During heated arguments where I feel unheard.

### Q7. When I switch to passive-aggressive communication
- When I am upset but do not feel safe expressing it directly.
- If I think raising the issue will be dismissed or mocked.

### Q8. Making my communication assertive
- Watch my tone and choose “I” statements to express needs clearly.
- Prepare key points and practice calm delivery before difficult talks.
- Set boundaries politely, then follow through with consistent actions.
