# Relaxation, Stress & Healthy Habits 

## Question 1: What are the activities you do that make you relax (Calm quadrant)?

- Listening to music
- Taking a walk without phone
- Lying down and closing my eyes
- Taking a warm bath
- Watching something light or funny
- Deep breathing or short meditation
- Spending quiet time alone

---

## Question 2: When do you find getting into the Stress quadrant?

- When exams or deadlines are near
- While learning something difficult or new
- During intense workouts
- When multitasking or rushing
- When expectations are high
- When overthinking future results

---

## Question 3: How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?

- I feel motivated and energized
- Time passes very fast
- I enjoy the task even if it is challenging
- I feel focused and curious
- I want to continue without forcing myself
- There is pressure, but it feels positive

---

## Question 4: Paraphrase – *Sleep is Your Superpower* 

- Sleep improves memory
- Sleep boosts learning ability
- Sleep improves focus and attention
- Poor sleep affects mood and emotions
- Sleep strengthens immunity
- Sleep helps physical recovery
- Lack of sleep increases health problems

---

## Question 5: What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?

- Fixed sleep and wake-up time
- No phone before sleeping
- Reduce caffeine at night
- Keep room dark and quiet
- Do light stretching or breathing
- Avoid heavy meals before bed
- Sleep and wake at same time daily

---

## Question 6: Paraphrase – *Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise* 

- Exercise improves brain memory
- Exercise reduces stress and anxiety
- Physical activity helps brain growth
- Exercise improves concentration
- It reduces chances of depression
- Exercise boosts mood chemicals
- It improves learning ability

---

## Question 7: What are some steps you can take to exercise more?

- Start with short walks daily
- Do light exercise at home
- Fix a daily exercise time
- Choose activities you enjoy
- Use stairs instead of lift
- Exercise with friends
- Keep goals small and realistic
